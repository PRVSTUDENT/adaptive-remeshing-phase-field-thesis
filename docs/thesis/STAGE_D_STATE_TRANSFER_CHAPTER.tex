\chapter{Controlled State Transfer for Phase-Field UEL Workflows}
\label{ch:stage-d-state-transfer}

\section{Objective}
Stage D tests whether phase-field and history values transferred between meshes
are actually ingested by Abaqus and routed through the UEL/UMAT state arrays.
The test is intentionally separated from fracture evolution. No interrupted
Molnar fracture simulation is allowed until the D2 ingestion, continuation,
thread-repeatability, and output-routing gates pass.

\section{D2A ingestion design}
D2A uses the prepared tiny nonmatching transfer package under
\texttt{models/state\_transfer/d2\_tiny\_transfer/}. A separate D2-only
UEL/UMAT source variant is generated under
\texttt{src/state\_transfer/d2\_tiny\_transfer\_uel.for}; the frozen Molnar
source and accepted C2C-v3 mesh are not modified.

The preserved Molnar U1 user-element card declares phase on Abaqus DOF~3. D2A
therefore prescribes every transferred nodal phase value on DOF~3. History
values are loaded from a generated read-only table keyed by physical element
label and integration point number, initialized once, and mirrored to
visualization \texttt{SDV16}. The interpolated phase value is mirrored to
\texttt{SDV15}.

\section{Current status}
The executable D2A package passed on HPC job \texttt{1376785.mmaster02}. The
PBS exit status was zero, the Abaqus solve completed successfully, the ODB was
readable, and \texttt{D2A.ok} was written. Target element/IP coverage was
$1.0$, the maximum \texttt{SDV15} interpolation error was $0.0$, and the
maximum \texttt{SDV16}/$H$ error was
$6.428999999030793\times10^{-9}$.

Abaqus did not expose the UEL phase DOF as usable nodal \texttt{U} output in
this smoke deck. The accepted D2A proof is therefore the element/IP UMAT mirror:
\texttt{SDV15} proves transferred phase ingestion, and \texttt{SDV16} proves
transferred history ingestion. This is an ingestion and routing verification,
not a fracture-response benchmark.

\section{D2B continuation attempt}
D2B was submitted once as serial PBS job \texttt{1376819.mmaster02}. It did
not pass. Abaqus compiled the D2-only user subroutine and entered the three-step
deck, but the tiny mechanical continuation terminated with solver exit $1$ and
PBS \texttt{Exit\_status=10}. The message file reports
\texttt{TOO MANY INCREMENTS NEEDED TO COMPLETE THE STEP}. Therefore no
\texttt{D2B.ok} marker exists, and no serial state-persistence acceptance claim
is made from this attempt.

The prepared corrective deck increases the release and continuation step
increment allowance to \texttt{inc=50}. It leaves the transferred phase values,
history values, transfer table, label mapping, and D2-only UEL/UMAT state route
unchanged.

\section{D2B accepted rerun}
The corrected D2B rerun, \texttt{1376825.mmaster02}, passed with PBS exit
status zero, solver exit zero, readable ODB output, and classification
\texttt{stage\_d2b\_serial\_continuation\_pass}. The target element/IP
coverage was $1.0$. The maximum initial differences from accepted D2A were
$0.0$ for both \texttt{SDV15} and \texttt{SDV16}; the maximum release
differences were also $0.0$. The final continuation frame reached
$U_2=1.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{mm}$ with finite \texttt{RF2}
$=3.46317381026\times10^{-7}$. The reported \texttt{ALLWK} continuation jump was
$1.2830926425511091\times10^{-11}$; no energy-jump acceptance tolerance is
assigned at D2B.

The rerun wrote \texttt{D2B\_R1.ok}, and the canonical \texttt{D2B.ok} marker
records the accepted rerun job ID.

\section{D2C four-thread repeatability}
The D2C job, \texttt{1376831.mmaster02}, reused the accepted D2B transfer deck
with no physics, transfer-table, mesh, label, step, increment-control, or target
displacement changes. The only execution change was from one serial thread to
\texttt{cpus=4}, \texttt{mp\_mode=threads}, and four OpenMP threads. The Abaqus
message output confirmed \texttt{1 MPI RANK x 4 THREAD}.

D2C passed with PBS exit status zero, solver exit zero, readable ODB output, and
classification \texttt{stage\_d2c\_thread\_repeatability\_pass}. Against the
D2B serial reference, the maximum \texttt{SDV15} and \texttt{SDV16}
thread--serial differences were both $0.0$, target element/IP coverage was
$1.0$, the final $U_2$ difference was $0.0\,\mathrm{mm}$, the final
\texttt{RF2} absolute and relative differences were both $0.0$, the RF--U NRMSE
was $0.0$, and the final-frame \texttt{ALLWK} absolute difference was $0.0$.
The accepted and cutback increment sequence was unchanged.

D2D ABAQUSER output-route verification is the next controlled gate.

\section{D2D0 ABAQUSER availability}
The D2D0 login-node audit did not find an ABAQUSER executable, module, source
implementation, or documented runnable interface. The audit therefore classified
D2D as \texttt{stage\_d2d\_blocked\_abaquser\_not\_found}. This is an
external-tool availability block, not a failure of D1--D2C state transfer. A
normal Abaqus Python extractor is not an acceptable substitute for ABAQUSER
verification.

The first interrupted fracture-transfer experiment has been prepared only as a
design, using a small H0 diagnostic model and an early pre-peak checkpoint near
$U=0.003\,\mathrm{mm}$. No D2D PBS job or interrupted fracture solver job has
been submitted.

\section{D3A existing H0 checkpoint audit}
The existing accepted H0 ODB from job \texttt{1376154.mmaster02} was tested as
the D3 checkpoint source. A CAE/ODB-only extraction selected the frame at
$U_2=0.003000000026077032\,\mathrm{mm}$ and extracted 15720 element/IP state
rows with full coverage, finite \texttt{SDV15}/\texttt{SDV16}, maximum
$d=0.08412302285432816$, maximum $H=0.0512588769197464$, checkpoint
\texttt{RF2} $=0.39450356364250183$, and
\texttt{RF2}/\texttt{RF2}_{\mathrm{peak,H0}}=0.5421925638518931$.

This is useful partial evidence but not an accepted D3A checkpoint. The source
ODB lacks \texttt{ALLIE}, \texttt{ALLSE}, and \texttt{ALLWK} energy history
outputs, so the D3A acceptance gate fails and no \texttt{D3A.ok} marker is
written. No D3A2 transfer package or fracture solver job was submitted.

\section{D3A-E independent energy reconstruction}
D3A-E attempted to replace unavailable global ODB energy history with an
independent quadrature reconstruction from the accepted H0 checkpoint exports.
The CAE/ODB-only job \texttt{1376885.mmaster02} reconstructed external work
$0.0005994580560459379$, bulk energy from \texttt{SDV12}
$0.0005918856529030076$, bulk energy from $0.5S:E$
$0.0005918856524092704$, total fracture energy
$2.280704034757352\times 10^{-8}$, total reconstructed internal energy
$0.0005919084599433551$, and relative energy residual
$0.012594035606728515$. The direct \texttt{SDV12} integration and independent
$0.5S:E$ integration agreed to relative difference
$8.341766862422363\times 10^{-10}$.

The energy route is not accepted yet. Validation failed on the predeclared
positive-Jacobian gate because five Jacobian determinants computed from the H0
deck connectivity were non-positive. The classification is
\path{stage_d3a_energy_reconstruction_fail}; no \texttt{D3A.ok} marker exists
and D3A2 remains blocked.
